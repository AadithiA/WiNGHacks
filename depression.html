What is Depression

Depression is a mental health condition characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest or pleasure in activities once enjoyed. It can also involve physical symptoms such as changes in appetite or sleep patterns, and in severe cases, may lead to thoughts of self-harm or suicide.

Signs and Symptom

  • Persistent Sadness
  • Loss of Interest or Pleasure
  • Changes in Sleep Patterns
  • Impulsive Behavior
  • Fatigue or Lack of Energy

    • Note:Expereincing one or two of the above symptoms doesn't necessarily mean depression. If they presist for more than 2 weeks and affect daily life, seekk help from a healthcare professional.

    Reaching Out

    If you or someone you know is experiencing symptoms of depression, don't hesitate to reach out for support. Hotlines, counseling services, and mental health professionals are available to provide guidance, empathy, and resources to help navigate through difficult times.

    More About Depression

  • An estimated 21.0 million adults in the United States had at least one major depressive episode. This number represented 8.3% of all U.S. adults.
  • The prevalence of major depressive episode was higher among adult females (10.3%) compared to males (6.2%).
  • The prevalence of major depressive episode was higher among adolescent females (29.2%) compared to males (11.5%).
  • Among adolescents with major depressive episode with severe impairment, an estimated 44.2% received treatment in the past year.
