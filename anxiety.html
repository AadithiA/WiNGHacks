    • Reaching Out

    If you're struggling with anxiety, remember that support is available. Don't hesitate to reach out to hotlines, counseling services, or mental health professionals who can provide guidance, reassurance, and coping strategies to help manage your anxiety.

    More About Anxiety

  • An estimated 19.1% of U.S. adults had any anxiety disorder in the past year.
  • Past year prevalence of any anxiety disorder was higher for females (23.4%) than for males (14.3%).
  • The prevalence of any anxiety disorder among adolescents was higher for females (38.0%) than for males (26.1%)
  • An estimated 31.1% of U.S. adults experience any anxiety disorder at some time in their lives

    • Dealing with Anxiety

    Everyone has different anxiety disorders, and it is important to know what to do when you are experiencing one. Anxiety disorders are unique for each person, and they can be hard to deal with. Here are some tips to help you deal with anxiety.

  • Seek Support: Talk to a trusted friend, family member, or mental health professional about your feelings and concerns. Sharing your experiences with others can provide comfort, validation, and helpful coping strategies.
  • Stay Active: Regular physical activity, such as walking, jogging, yoga, or dancing, can help reduce stress and anxiety by releasing endorphins and improving overall mood.
  • Journaling: Keeping track of your day can make it more easy to organize thoughts
  • Healthy Lifestyle Choices Maintain a balanced diet, get adequate sleep, limit caffeine and alcohol intake, and avoid recreational drugs, as these substances can exacerbate anxiety symptoms. Establishing healthy habits can support overall well-being and help manage anxiety.
